Two worlds of Optimal Transport

For two decades OT has grown along two nearly separate tracks.

geometry Wasserstein geometry

  • Wasserstein distance \(W_2\)
  • Geodesics & barycenters
  • Gradient flows

What geometry does OT induce on probability space?

computation Computational OT

  • Sinkhorn · Sliced \(\mathrm{SW}_2\)
  • Low-rank · Minibatch
  • Neural OT

How do we compute OT at scale?

Two communities, two vocabularies — but no shared yardstick linking approximation quality to Wasserstein geometry. How far is a fast solution from the truth?

The missing quantity: the duality gap

Kantorovich duality brackets the true cost — their difference is computable without ever solving exact OT.

Duality gap \(G\)
A computable certificate of distance to true \(\mathrm{OT}^\star\) — never needs \(\mathrm{OT}^\star\) itself.
Upper bound \(U\)
Cost of any feasible primal coupling \(\pi\in\Pi(\mu,\nu)\).
Lower bound \(L\)
Value of any dual-feasible potential pair \((f,g)\in\Phi_c\).

Every method controls one side of \(G\)

relaxation raises \(L\)

  • Sliced OT (avg 1-D dual)
  • Semi-dual / Dual OT
  • Neural potentials + margin correction

shrinks the dual deficit \(\;\mathrm{OT}^\star-L\)

restriction lowers \(U\)

  • Low-rank OT (sub-coupling)
  • Minibatch OT (point-cloud)
  • Map rounding (Brenier-feasible)

shrinks the primal excess \(\;U-\mathrm{OT}^\star\)

The duality principle: a method either raises \(L\) (relaxation), lowers \(U\) (restriction), or both — only the latter gives a certified two-sided gap.

Why fast OT usually loses geometry

Fidelity–cost frontier measured on Gaussian ground truth, \(n=3{,}000\), \(d=16\), 5 seeds.
Sliced OT plateaus — it cannot reach low error regardless of cost. Certified methods (Sinkhorn) need both bounds to converge.

Theorem 1 — Generalized dimensional deficit

Theorem 1

(i) Common covariance (exact). For \(\mu=\mathcal N(a,\Sigma)\), \(\nu=\mathcal N(b,\Sigma)\), any SPD \(\Sigma\):

$$\frac{W_2^2-\mathrm{SW}_2^2}{W_2^2}=1-\tfrac{1}{d}$$

(ii) Anisotropic lower bound. For any \(\mu=\mathcal N(a,\Sigma_0)\), \(\nu=\mathcal N(b,\Sigma_1)\), letting \(\rho=\|m\|^2/W_2^2\):

$$\frac{W_2^2-\mathrm{SW}_2^2}{W_2^2}\;\ge\;\Bigl(1-\tfrac{1}{d}\Bigr)\rho$$

Answers the "isotropic only" critique — the floor holds for any covariance structure; grows as \(d\to\infty\).

Deficit tracks \(1-1/d\) across dimension (common-\(\Sigma\) Gaussians, \(n=3{,}000\)).
No matter the covariance — isotropic or anisotropic — sliced OT loses at least \((1-1/d)\) of Wasserstein geometry.

Theorem 2 — Certification is hard in general

Theorem 2 — Ω(n²) barrier

In the entry-query model, any (randomized) algorithm that outputs a valid certified gap \(G\le\delta\) (for \(\delta<\tfrac14\)) for every \(C\in[0,1]^{n\times n}\) must make \(\Omega(n^2)\) cost evaluations.

Proof sketch (planted-matching adversary):

  • ① Answer every queried entry = 1
  • ② If < \(n^2/16\) queries: Hall's theorem guarantees a permutation \(\sigma\) avoiding all queried cells
  • ③ Plant \(C[i,\sigma(i)]=0\); all others \(=1\) → \(\mathrm{OT}^\star=0\)
  • ④ Algorithm cannot distinguish from "all-ones" → gap uncertified

escape Structured costs

Sliced (\(O(n)\) projections), low-rank (\(O(n)\)), entropic (\(O(n^2)\) but GPU-fast, certified) — exploit structure to escape the lower bound.

insight Memory wall (observed)

On an 85 GB A100: float64 OOM at \(n=55{,}000\), float32 OOM at \(n=80{,}000\). Certifying general dense OT is not just hard in theory — it hits the physical memory wall in practice.

Certification for general dense costs is provably Ω(n²) — structure (sliced, low-rank, entropic) is not a convenience; it is a necessity.

A constructive recipe: the Composed Gap Certificate

Any valid lower-bound oracle plus any valid upper-bound oracle yields a certified bracket — at near-linear cost.

Cost

\(\mathrm{cost}(\mathcal L)+\mathrm{cost}(\mathcal U)\) — both oracles run independently, near-linear in \(n\).

Calibration

More projections / larger batch / higher rank → tighter \(G\) at higher cost. Plug-and-play.

Failure mode

Dimensional deficit (Thm 1) sets a structural floor for \(\mathcal L=\)Sliced. Upgrade to entropic lower bound to escape.

Composition is the key: two cheap one-sided estimates, run independently, produce a valid two-sided certificate without \(O(n^2)\) dense computation.

Summary

We propose a single coordinate — the certified Kantorovich gap \(G=U-L\) — that organizes all approximate OT methods on one scale.

concept

\(G=U-L\) as a cross-family, computable fidelity coordinate — without OT*.

theory

Thm 1: sliced deficit \(\ge1-1/d\) for any Gaussian family. Thm 2: certifying \(G\le\delta\) costs \(\Omega(n^2)\) in general.

algorithm

CGC: compose any \(\mathcal L\) + any \(\mathcal U\) → valid two-sided certificate at near-linear cost.

evidence

Gap 100% valid; gap-tol = oracle on both tasks; deficit confirmed in PCA, diffmap, raw gene space.

The duality gap is the missing coordinate that converts approximate OT from a collection of heuristics into a certifiable, comparable science.
